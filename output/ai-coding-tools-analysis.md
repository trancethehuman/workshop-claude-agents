## CRAFT: 5 Key Insights with Supporting Evidence

### 1. AI coding is the fastest-scaling SaaS category in history

Cursor reached $1B ARR in ~24 months — faster than any prior SaaS company. Replit grew revenue 53x in a single year. This isn't a niche; it's a platform shift comparable to cloud computing.

> **Evidence:** Cursor $1B ARR (Nov 2025); Replit $2.8M → $150M annualized; database shows 27x sector funding growth 2020-2025.

### 2. The market is bifurcating into "pro developer" and "citizen developer" tracks

Cursor dominates professional developers (1M+ daily active, majority of Fortune 500). Replit owns the "vibe coder" — non-engineers building apps through natural language. These are complementary, not competing, markets.

> **Evidence:** Cursor: 50,000+ teams, Fortune 500 focus. Replit: "vibe coding" narrative, mobile app development, 150K+ customers.

### 3. Agentic coding (not autocomplete) is the winning paradigm

Every major player pivoted from code completion to autonomous agents in 2025. The question shifted from "suggest the next line" to "complete the entire task."

> **Evidence:** Cursor Background Agents + BugBot; Replit Agent 3 (200 min autonomous sessions); Windsurf Cascade + AI Flows.

### 4. Investor concentration signals high conviction

Google Ventures leads 3 companies in this database subsector. Khosla Ventures is the largest investor by dollars ($76.6M). Nvidia and Google joined Cursor's $2.3B round as strategic investors.

> **Evidence:** Database shows GV leading Codegen, Continue, and Codeium rounds. Real-world rounds attract strategic capital (Nvidia, Google).

### 5. Consolidation is accelerating — the standalone window is closing

Cursor acquired 3 companies in 2025. Cognition AI acquired Windsurf assets. OpenAI attempted to acquire Cursor. The land grab is well underway.

> **Evidence:** 5+ M&A events in AI coding in 2025 alone. New entrants face incumbents with $1B+ war chests.
